12.10 Compare the throughput achieved by a RAID Level 5 organization with that achieved by a RAID Level 1 organization for the following:

  1. Read operations on single blocks
  2. Read operations on multiple contiguous blocks

RAID 1 simply mirrors a disk while RAID 3 combines every disk into one continues logical volume while sacrificing one disk to use as a parity disk. RAID 5 is exactly like RAID 3 except it distributes the parity slice between every disk in the volume (to avoid excess read/writes on the parity disk causing it to fail first).

RAID 5 will provide better throughput for contiguous reads because requests can be pulled simultaneous from three or more disks. RAID 1 is very similar, however it can only "stripe" reads from at most two disks. As long as the RAID 5 is setup on an interface that allows for simultaneous I/O requests (SCSI, SATA or Parallel IDE with multiple controllers), the read performance of RAID 5 will surpass that of RAID 1. It's distribution of parity data and the storage capacity of size * (n-1) where n is the number of disks in the array makes RAID 5 the most economical of the major RAID configurations.
